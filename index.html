Trump and the Peace Prize

Based on reports from early 2026, Venezuelan opposition leader María Corina Machado presented her 2025 Nobel Peace Prize medal to U.S. President Donald Trump at the White House. Machado justified this action as recognition for Trump’s role in the military intervention to remove Nicolás Maduro from power. The Nobel Committee stated the medal, but not the title, was transferred. Key details regarding the situation:

  • Symbolic Gift: Machado, a 2025 Nobel laureate, gifted her physical medal to Trump in January 2026, claiming she had "no regrets" despite backlash, noting her appreciation for his action in Venezuela.
  • Medal Transfer Limits: The Norwegian Nobel Committee clarified that the medal itself can be given away, but the official Nobel Prize title and status cannot be transferred, revoked, or shared.
  • Political Context: The move was criticized by some Norwegian politicians as "absurd". The gift followed a U.S. special forces operation in Venezuela.
  • FIFA Peace Prize: In a separate development, FIFA awarded President Trump the inaugural "FIFA Peace Prize – Football Unites the World" at the 2026 World Cup draw in Washington D.C., presented by Gianni Infantino.
  • Previous Nominations: Trump was previously nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize in 2025 and 2026 by various political figures.
